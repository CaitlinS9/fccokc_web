Attending Your First Meetup

Thinking about coming to your first FreeCodeCamp OKC meetup? Congratulations! You've taken the first step on your journey in learning programming.

Going to a meetup can be intimidating at first, but it doesn't have to be. Here are some tips for attending your first meetup.

What Should I Bring?

There are no required materials to attend a FreeCodeCamp meetup. You may want to bring some pen and paper to take notes during talks. Many of our members also bring their laptops but they are not a necessity as we do not do much coding during talks.

What To Do When Attending

Show up a few minutes early. This will give you a chance to meet and network with people before the talk starts. You will also have a few minutes after the talk for chatting and networking. We do need to vacate the event space on time, but feel free to carry on outside or elsewhere.

Our talks will be mostly concept-based talks with very little coding involved, so a laptop is not necessary, but feel free to bring one if you have one. Most of your coding will be done at home. Feel free to bring any questions you might have and ask before or after the talks, or ask other members on Slack

Remember to be polite and respectful and keep the Code of Conduct in mind at FreeCodeCamp and any other Techlahoma meetups. Also remember to act professionally, as many members could very easily be your future coworkers, managers, or interviewers, so networking is very important.
